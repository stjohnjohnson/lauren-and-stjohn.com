Our Story

So how did you guys meet?

It all started when we both attended the same college, Sacred Heart University in Fairfield, CT. By pure chance, we ended up living in the same freshman dorm only three floors apart. If that wasn't enough, it turned out we even had a common acquaintance, Lauren's roommate.

With so much luck on our side, it wasn't long before we became great friends. Watching movie marathons, ordering late-night Chinese food, and chatting until dawn - absolutely inseparable. To no one's surprise, our friendship quickly flourished into a romance, and the rest is history.

How did you end up here in California?

Throughout our relationship, we talked about someday moving to California. And once we both finished grad school, we realized that this was the perfect time to look into moving. Little did we know how incredibly soon this would really happen.

St. John did some looking around for jobs. And after a few interviews with Yahoo, BAM! We were packed up and moved out to sunny California in under a month!

Alright, how did you pop the question?

After living in California for only a week, we decided to spend a day (August 7th, 2010) exploring Napa Valley with a bunch of friends visiting from Connecticut. Our final stop on our personal wine tour was the beautiful Castello di Amorosa.

By that time, Lauren was a bit tipsy (luckily) and I (St. John) was able to convince her to come explore the top of the hill with me. After strolling through the grape vines, overlooking the castle, I got down on one knee, proposed, and she said YES!

Wedding



How did you choose this wedding venue?

Well, since we were new to the area, we had to start fresh. So we spent a couple months looking at venues all around the area. We looked far and wide at all different wineries, mansions, wedding halls, golf clubs, and even the occasional retreat. After much deliberation, we compromised on a site down in Saratoga. It was beautiful, had amazing views, but somehow, it just didn't feel "right." And by this point we had exhausted almost every venue within an hour and a half drive. But on a whim, we decided to cancel our reservation and keep looking.

I thought you looked everywhere? Where else would you go?

It was one of the last venues on our list, the Thomas Fogarty Winery, and it had amazing reviews. But for some reason or another, we had not had a chance to check it out yet. So, we made an appointment with hopes that it would be "the place."

Sounds promising! How did it go?

The day of our visit had absolutely abysmal weather. It was gloomy, foggy, and pouring rain. But the second we turned off the road and drove through the gates, we both knew this was the place for our wedding. It was absolutely breath-taking, even in the worst weather.

Wonderful! Congratulations!

Since our wedding will be at the end of May, we hope to have a stunning, gorgeous, sunny day and can’t wait for our guests to experience the beauty of this vineyard!

Weather

Can be really unpredictable. It may be California, but we're on top of a mountain - so more than likely it'll be windy and a bit chilly. We won't really know the specifics until closer to the day. But bring something warm just in case.

Attire

Guys, keep it simple, Suit and Tie (or Sport Jacket). Gals, you already know, Dresses.

Driving

Under NO circumstance should you drive on Old La Honda road. Avoid it at all costs - even if your GPS says otherwise. It's a windy, two-way, one-lane, no guardrail, dirt trail. Instead you should be taking Highway 84 - AKA La Honda road (notice it's not old). Best directions are on Thomas Fogarty's website - available here.
